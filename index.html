Why It Matters

This Is About More Than a Career

The public workforce evolution isn't just about jobs. It's about the person who spent their career serving others finally having the time, the freedom, and the financial architecture to be present — for school pickups, for family dinners, for the life they put on hold.

Not everyone has the opportunity to leverage savings or severance to upskill and rebuild. That's exactly why this bridge needs to exist.

For nearly 50 years, the public workforce has lived with chronic funding uncertainty. Since 1976, Congress has created 27 funding gaps, leading to 11 shutdowns that furloughed hundreds of thousands of workers. The last time all appropriations bills passed on time? 1997. That's nearly three decades of the workforce sitting through furloughs, shutdowns, continuing resolutions, and the constant anxiety of "will I get paid next month?"

It is exhausting and destabilizing to wait for another furlough, another shutdown, another round of layoffs. The public workforce evolution is here — and it's being led by people who lived it.

The Founder

Built From the Inside

Carmen S. Artis didn't study the public workforce from the outside. She spent 17 years inside it — as both a public service contractor and a federal employee.

Working within HHS and complex regulatory environments, Carmen operated at the center of multimillion-dollar oversight systems, procurement frameworks, and cross-agency governance. She knows what it takes to navigate government bureaucracy — and she has spoken to and positively impacted government transitioners navigating the effects of leaving, whether voluntary or involuntary, and some without a plan.

She built the bridge that wasn't available during her own transition from government service — to ensure the work-life and family balance that every public servant deserves but very few master.

But what she found on the other side wasn't a gap in her own career — it was a systemic gap. Not everyone has the opportunity to leverage savings or severance to upskill. Thousands of highly skilled government professionals were exiting with no infrastructure to convert their expertise into independent work.

Beyond The Buyout™ is her response.
